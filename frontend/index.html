Discover Our City

Bhairahawa (also known as Siddharthanagar) is a gateway to one of Nepal's most prized heritage sites, Lumbini, and serves as an important Nepal-India border crossing at Belahiya.

What is Bhairahawa famous for?

Bhairahawa is renowned for its cultural diversity and as the main entry point to Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha. The city has grown into an important commercial and transportation hub with an international airport connecting visitors from around the world.

  • Cultural diversity and vibrant markets
  • Gateway to UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • International airport connectivity
  • Warm hospitality and local cuisine

Rani Mahal

Near Dhakdhai

Rani Mahal, also known as the "Taj Mahal of Nepal," is a historic palace located on the banks of the Kali Gandaki River in Palpa district. It was built in 1897 by General Khadga Shamsher Rana in memory of his beloved wife, Tej Kumari Devi. The palace features a blend of neoclassical and European architectural styles and stands as a symbol of love and loss.

Siddha Baba Mandir

Temple

Siddha Baba Temple is a famous Hindu pilgrimage site located near Butwal in Rupandehi district, Nepal. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is situated along the Siddhababa Hill on the East-West Highway. Devotees visit the temple to offer prayers, especially on Mondays and during Shivaratri, believing that wishes made here are fulfilled. The temple is also known for its peaceful natural surroundings.
